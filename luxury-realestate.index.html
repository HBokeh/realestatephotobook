Industry Overview

Real estate photography sits at the intersection of technical precision and artistic vision. Unlike portrait or event photography, you are selling a feeling — the warmth of morning light spilling across hardwood floors, the expansive openness of a properly composed living room.

"The difference between a $200 shoot and a $2,000 shoot is not the camera body — it's the photographer's understanding of how light, composition, and timing conspire to make a space feel inevitable."

Full-Frame vs APS-C

Full-frame delivers a decisive advantage: a 16mm lens captures a 107° horizontal field of view vs. 24mm-equivalent on APS-C. For luxury work, full-frame is non-negotiable. APS-C earns its place as a backup body — lighter for long shoot days, more affordable, and capable of professional results in the mid-market segment.

RAW vs JPEG — No Debate

Shoot RAW. Always. JPEG permanently discards the majority of data your sensor captures. For real estate photography — where you must recover blown highlights AND lift deep shadows in the same image — JPEG simply cannot deliver what's needed. RAW gives you up to 14 stops of recoverable dynamic range.

ISO Guidelines
100Always prefer
200Acceptable
400Luxury limit
800+Avoid
Aperture Sweet Spot

For real estate, the aperture goal is edge-to-edge sharpness. Most quality wide lenses achieve their sharpest output between f/8 and f/11. Below this, aberrations reduce sharpness. Above f/16, diffraction sets in — counter-intuitively making images less sharp when stopped down further.

The Flicker Problem

In North America, fluorescent and LED lights cycle at 120Hz. Always shoot at multiples of 1/125s for indoor artificial light to avoid inconsistent brightness across your bracket sequence.

Twilight & Blue Hour

"Twilight photography is where preparation meets opportunity. The house that looks adequate at noon can look extraordinary at blue hour — but only if you've spent 45 minutes setting up before the sky turns."

Civil Twilight

Orange → pink sky. WB: 4500K. Warm dramatic atmosphere.

Nautical ★

Deep blue sky. WB: 3200K. The classic luxury look. 15–20 min window.

Astronomical

Navy → dark blue. Very dramatic. Sky quickly goes black — act fast.

Multi-Flash Setup Key Rules
  • Use Godox radio triggers — place units behind furniture, outside frame
  • Start all flash units at 1/8 power, increase from there after test shot
  • Gel all units to match ambient colour temperature (typically 1/2–1 CTO)
  • The camera's on-unit flash must fire to trigger remotes even at 1/128 power

Final Walkthrough — 5-Step Process

  1. Walk every room with your phone camera — the lens reveals what the eye filters out. Small objects you don't consciously register will be clearly visible in your final images.

  2. Check every mirror and reflective surface for your reflection path before unpacking any gear.

  3. Turn on every light and verify all bulbs function. A single non-working sconce ruins a symmetrical bedroom shot.

  4. Set ceiling fans off. Fan blades blur at any shutter speed you'll use in interiors — slower than 1/500s.

  5. Check all windows for cleanliness from the interior. Carry glass cleaner and microfiber in your kit.

Decluttering Standards by Room

Kitchen: max 3 items on any countertop. Bedroom: nightstands — one lamp, one decorative item. Bathroom: completely clear except soap dispenser, small plant, and white hand towel.

Chapter Seven
The Outer Shell
Exterior Photography
Workflow
Front Elevation Strategy

Walk the entire street frontage before setting up. The hero angle for most properties is 30–45° off centre — slightly left or right creates perspective and dimension that a dead-centre shot never achieves. Use 24mm on full-frame as the professional standard for residential facades.

Pool Photography
🏊

CPL Filter is Non-Negotiable for Pools. A circular polariser eliminates surface glare that makes water appear opaque. Without it: flat white reflections. With it: transparent blue depth showing the pool bottom. Rotate the filter until reflections disappear. This single filter change transforms every pool photo you take.

Twilight Exterior Checklist
  • Arrive 45–60 min before sunset — lock composition before sky turns
  • Turn on every interior light in the house — ceiling, sconces, all practicals
  • Set WB to 3200K — interior glows amber, sky becomes rich blue
  • Best images: 15–25 min after sunset — shoot continuously
  • Use mirror lockup and remote shutter — 3–8 sec exposures need zero vibration

Chapter Eight

Composition

6 Tools for Depth Creation
Tool 1
Scale Comparison

Objects of known size (chairs, doors) appearing at different distances create instant depth reference.

Tool 2
Converging Lines

Floor boards, ceiling beams, baseboard lines converging toward the distance — strongest depth signal.

Tool 3
Overlapping Elements

When a near object partially covers a far object, depth is immediately perceived. Chair arm over sofa.

Tool 4
Light Falloff

Natural vignetting in corners draws the eye inward and creates the sensation of entering the space.

"Minimalist framing asks: what is the single most important element of this space, and how do I let it breathe? This is the compositional philosophy that defines luxury photography."

The Centering Test

Before every symmetrical shot: draw an imaginary vertical line down the centre. Every element on the left should have a visual counterpart on the right. Either perfectly symmetrical or deliberately asymmetrical — never accidentally unbalanced.

Flambient Workflow
Exposure 1 — Ambient

No flash. Expose for room interior. Windows will blow out — correct. ISO 100, f/8, shutter as needed.

Exposure 2 — Flash

Add ceiling-bounce flash. Underexpose ambient -1.5 EV by raising shutter speed. Flash fills dark areas cleanly.

⚡

Luminosity Blend Mode Secret: Set flash layer to Luminosity in Photoshop — not Normal. This blends only brightness values, not colour, preserving the room's warm ambient tone while flash adds only its light. The difference between a result that looks natural and one that looks processed.

Mixed Lighting Fix

The professional nuclear option when lighting is unmanageable: turn off every artificial light and shoot purely natural-light brackets. Eliminates all colour-temperature conflicts instantly. Use flash to replace missing fill.

Window Overexposure Recovery
Prevention

Always bias bracket toward underexposure. If window still blows at -4 EV, go to -6.

Lightroom HDR

Merge 5-frame bracket. Auto Settings off. Deghost Medium. Process merged DNG.

Window Pull

Separate -3 EV exposure masked in Photoshop. Highest quality for premium views.
